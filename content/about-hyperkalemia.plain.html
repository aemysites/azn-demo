About Hyperkalemia

Hyperkalemia is an electrolyte disorder1

Predisposing factors:1-5

HF
T2D
CKD
Concomitant
RAASi
use

In studies, the incidence of hyperkalemia over follow-up duration of approximately 1 year was observed to be:6

<5%
with
HF

Up to
40%
with
chronic HF

~73%
with
CKD

Diabetes Canada guidelines recommend sodium zirconium
cyclosilicate as a treatment option in patients with moderate to
high potassium1
KDIGO guidelines recommend potassium binders as
a treatment option for RAASi-induced hyperkalemia7*

* Practice points are consensus-based statements representing the expert judgment of the Work Group and are not graded. Although these statements are developed based on a different methodology, they should not be seen as "less important" or a "downgrade" from graded recommendations.7

CKD=chronic kidney disease; HF=heart failure; RAASi=Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system inhibitor; T2D=Type 2 diabetes.
